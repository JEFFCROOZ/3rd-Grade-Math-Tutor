# 01 — Bridge Topics Reference

This file maps the two-lane summer bridge at a high level.

## Grade 3 Review

### 3.OA — Multiplication & Division Review

Big idea: equal groups, arrays, multiplication facts, and division as a related idea rather than an unrelated new operation.

### 3.NBT — Place Value to 1,000 Review

Big idea: place value supports rounding, addition, subtraction, and early multiplication by multiples of 10.

### 3.NF — Fractions Review

Big idea: fractions are numbers that represent equal parts of a whole and can be placed on a number line.

### 3.MD — Measurement & Data Review

Big idea: area connects back to multiplication, perimeter measures boundary, and graphs organize real information.

### 3.G — Shapes Review

Big idea: shapes have attributes, and equal parts connect geometry back to fractions.

## Grade 4 Preview

### 4.OA — Operations & Problem Solving

Big idea: fourth grade expects students to compare quantities multiplicatively, solve multi-step word problems, and reason about factors, multiples, and patterns.

### 4.NBT — Place Value & Big Numbers

Big idea: place value expands far beyond 1,000, and students begin using that structure to support multi-digit arithmetic.

### 4.NF — Fractions & Decimals

Big idea: fractions become a major focus. Students compare equivalent fractions, add and subtract with like denominators, multiply a whole number by a fraction, and begin connecting fractions to decimals.

### 4.MD — Measurement, Data & Angles

Big idea: measurement now includes conversions, more complex problem solving, line plots with fractional data, and the introduction of angle measure.

### 4.G — Geometry Preview

Big idea: geometry becomes more classification-heavy. Students identify lines, angles, triangles, quadrilaterals, and lines of symmetry by properties.

## Product Use

The app should use the Grade 3 lane to rebuild confidence and the Grade 4 lane to reduce surprise.

This is the practical tutoring stance:

- use Grade 3 Review when accuracy is shaky or fluency is missing
- use Grade 4 Preview when the student is ready to stretch without overload
- switch between the two based on progress, not pride
